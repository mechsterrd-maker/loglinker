Module · Production & shop floor

Shot logging that takes 30 seconds

Operator on the shop floor, on their phone:
  • Pick die → part auto-filled from die master
  • Speak qty in Tamil → AI fills the number
  • Tap rejection types from a per-stage dropdown (no free text)
  • Snap a photo if there's a quality issue
  • Submit → die strokes update · BOM deducts raw stock · OEE refreshes · daily Pareto rebuilds · All Hands gets a one-line update
5 minutes at the office PC → 30 seconds on the floor. That's 4.5 min × 20 shots/day × 250 days = 375 hours/year.

📋 Shot count · good + per-stage reject breakdown
🔧 Die stroke counter · auto-incremented for life tracking
📦 Raw stock deduct · via BOM with scrap %
📅 Schedule progress · supplied-against-plan refreshed
📸 Attachments · photo + voice note + email reference
💬 All Hands · live cascade with actor + machine

Module · Documents · OCR · Bills

Snap the bill. Skip the typing.

Vendor sends a bill on WhatsApp. With Loglinkr that's now a 3-second flow:
  • Photo of bill is posted in chat — anyone, even ops
  • AI OCR extracts vendor name, GSTIN, items, qty, rate, tax, total, due date
  • Vendor master auto-matched by GSTIN + alias map (Simpson ASP vs Simpson SP — different units, same GSTIN, separately tracked)
  • GRN draft auto-created with line items → review and confirm
  • Stock auto-incremented on approval
  • Posted to Freedom AP via the two-hook sync (single source of truth for finance: Freedom)

Doc types Loglinkr handles

Vendor invoice (AP) Customer invoice (AR) DC in DC out Job-work DC out Job-work DC in Inter-unit transfer Bill Quote PO Payment receipt GRN

Bill ↔ Schedule mapping

When a bill has the customer's name, the supply auto-matches against the open schedule line. Part-number fuzzy match (tail-3-digit) handles ambiguous naming. Customer aliases (e.g. "BHEL" = "Bharat Heavy Electricals") configured once, applied forever.
Re-typing vendor bills costs ~30 min/day of accounts time. OCR brings this to under 5 minutes.
Module · Customer Schedules · Supplies

Plan vs supply, live — not monthly

What goes in

  • Customer schedule (email / PDF) — paste or photo, AI extracts week-wise plan
  • Outward DC / invoice — auto-counted as supply against the matching schedule line
  • Customer alias map — handles "ROOP Polymers" vs "ROOP" vs "Roop India"

What comes out

  • Live gap per part: planned − supplied
  • Urgency badge: 🟢 done · 🟡 due-soon · 🟠 due-today · 🔴 overdue
  • Home banner showing today's deliveries + overdue lines
  • Schedule Status card · current month progress

Bill Mapping master

For each customer, set up aliases once. From then on every OCR'd bill flows through to the right schedule line automatically. Zero monthly reconciliation.

Inter-unit transfers

Send 200 castings from Unit 1 to Unit 2 for finishing — the system handles both legs as one transfer event, prevents double-count, and rolls up correctly per customer.

Schedule Lines manager

Add / edit / split schedule lines. Add a part number to a line that didn't have one — every future bill matches automatically.
Module · Sub-Contracting · Job-work · Supplier Chase

Stop losing material at the vendor

🏭 Sub-Contracting

  • Send DC out · pick part + qty + vendor + process · SLA auto-fills expected return date
  • At-Vendor view grouped by vendor with 180-day GST clock (Sec 143 CGST) · red border = breach
  • Receive DC in with received + scrap + processing charge · auto-closes line
  • Conversion job-work · send ingot kg → receive insert pcs with yield ratio (1.05 kg/pc). Stock-in goes to the yield item, not the sent item.

🛒 Supplier Chase

  • For every open customer schedule, explodes through BOM → raw material demand for next 14 days
  • Compares with current stock + incoming POs
  • If short → critical / high / medium alert with the supplier name to chase
  • WhatsApp deep-link with pre-filled chase message per supplier
  • Daily 9:50 AM cron posts to All Hands + creates a chase task
Two problems vanish: (1) Material lying at vendor > 180 days = GST input credit reversal — auto-flagged. (2) Raw material shortage discovered when machine stops — pre-empted by 7-14 day runway alert.
Module · MOM · Action Hub · NPD/APQP

From meeting room to closed task — without a follow-up email

📝 MOM — Minutes of Meeting

  • Auto-numbered MOM/CMOM/SMOM per plant per month
  • Multi-point agenda — each point with its own responsible person + due date
  • Internal attendees as chip multi-select; customer / supplier-side attendees with email + role
  • Each point auto-creates a task in the responsible person's Action Hub
  • When the task closes → MOM action item auto-closes → when all points done, MOM auto-closes
  • Two-way sync: close the point manually, the task closes too

✓ Action Hub

Every task in one place — auto-spawned from NCR, breakdown, TPM critical fail, customer complaint, MOM, supplier alert, manual assignment. Each task carries the source context (linked NCR, machine, customer). Department filter. Daily 9 AM escalation if overdue.

🧪 NPD / APQP

  • RFQ → Concept → Design → Tooling → SOP phases with gates
  • Commercials, documents, decisions all in one project view
  • Auto-feeds PPAP submission on go-live
  • Daily pipeline summary to plant head
Meetings used to end with "we'll follow up via email." Now they end with 5 tasks already in 5 inboxes, and a closed MOM 2 weeks later when everyone delivers.
