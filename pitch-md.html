07 · cherry-picking is the trap

Production Entry + Task Manager — but those are 2 of 25 modules.

Each Loglinkr module carries its full weight only when the cascades around it are on. Cherry-picking gives you 20% of the gain.

Production Entry alone vs. with cascades on:

  • Shot logged → consumes BOM → updates raw stock → updates die strokes → forecasts next die change → feeds Pareto + OEE + reject rate KPI
  • Same shot → updates customer schedule supply if it's a finished good → updates supplier-side plan vs supply
  • Same shot → if rejected qty > threshold → auto-creates NCR with structured reason types

Task Manager alone vs. with cascades on:

  • Auto-spawns from NCR raised, breakdown reported, TPM critical fail, overdue stage log, customer complaint, audit finding
  • Each task arrives with the linked record's context — operator doesn't search, the task carries it
  • Overdue tasks escalate daily to plant head with the source record attached

Take 2 modules, leave the cascades off, and we're paying 2× for 20% of the value.

09 · the ask

One ask — a fair 30-day pilot.

What we turn on

  • One plant (start small — wdwdw or krishnas fittings)
  • All modules on, full cascades on
  • No parallel paper / no parallel Excel
  • Freedom continues for accounting unchanged

What we measure

  • Avg operator data-entry time (before / after)
  • Time from NCR raised to closure
  • Audit-readiness % (live IATF dashboard)
  • Operator NPS — "would you go back to paper?"

Hard go / no-go after 30 days. If the gain isn't seen, we revert. No penalty.
